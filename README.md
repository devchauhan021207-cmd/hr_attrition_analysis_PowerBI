# hr_attrition_analysis_PowerBI
HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard built using Power BI to identify employee turnover patterns and retention opportunities.
## Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns
- Identify high-risk departments and job roles
- Evaluate salary and overtime impact on attrition
- Support HR retention strategies
## Key KPIs

- Total Employees: 1470
- Attrition Count: 237
- Attrition Rate: 16.1%
- Average Age: 36.92
- Average Monthly Income: 6.50K

## Key Insights

### Department Analysis
- Research & Development recorded the highest attrition.
- Sales department showed the second-highest attrition.

### Job Role Analysis
- Laboratory Technicians experienced the highest turnover.
- Sales Executives and Research Scientists also showed elevated attrition.

### Salary Analysis
- Employees in the low salary slab showed significantly higher attrition.

### Overtime Analysis
- Employees working overtime represented the majority of attrition cases.

## Recommendations

### Improve Compensation
Review salary structures for lower-income employees.

### Reduce Overtime
Balance workloads and reduce employee burnout.


### Focus on High-Risk Roles
Create retention programs for Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives.

### Career Development
Provide growth opportunities for mid-career employees.
